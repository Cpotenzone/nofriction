Section 1: The Productization Imperative

Research Background

The evidence is irrefutable. Multiple studies [Martin, 2022; Porter, 2023] have revealed that the "valley of death" between promising R&D and successful real-world deployment isn't primarily caused by underfunding or market limitations—it's driven by systematic failures in productization.

The dual-use ecosystem—particularly in critical domains like AI, energy security, and cyber defense—continues to suffer from drastically under-resourced efforts to operationalize inventions. We develop cutting-edge prototypes but fail to engineer them into deployable assets that can withstand real-world conditions.

The recent CNAS report (2024) calls for production-readiness as an urgent national security priority. This paper not only concurs but goes further:

  • Productization is the force multiplier that bridges critical R&D to operational reality—it is not an afterthought but the essential process that determines success or failure.
  • Only dual-use technologies—those engineered from the ground up for both defense and civilian application—yield the resilience, scale, and adaptability required in today's volatile threat landscape [Toffler, 2023].

The No Friction Approach

"We must recut technology and remix the story to create defensible, needed products that solve tomorrow's challenges today." (The Technology Editor, No Friction, 2025).

Our methodology rejects the false choice between speed and substance through:

  • Immersive engagement with client teams to identify core use-cases and design requirements for ruggedization, scalability, and cross-domain exportability.
  • Hybrid expertise deployment that blends human productization architects with advanced agentic AI tools to achieve both speed and engineering rigor [No Friction, 2024].

Section 2: Dual Use Is The Only Use That Matters

Why Dual Use Is Non-Negotiable

History speaks clearly: defense sector innovation consistently catalyzes commercial breakthroughs—from GPS and the Internet to encryption and advanced materials (CNAS, 2024; RAND Corp, 2023). This isn't coincidence; it's the natural outcome when technologies are engineered for the most demanding use cases.

Designing for dual use from the outset ensures that products are:

  • Battle-tested under the most stringent conditions
  • Compliant by design with the highest regulatory standards
  • Built for durability and resilience against cascading failures
  • Architected for scale with clear pathways to mass adoption

"When we build only for civilian markets, we create fragility. When we build only for defense, we create isolation. When we build for both—with intention and rigor—we create resilience."

America + Europe + Allies: The Transatlantic Imperative

"Transatlantic codevelopment drives higher resilience and interoperable standards that can withstand both market and geopolitical shocks." [EIT Digital, 2023].

The emerging reality is that neither America nor Europe can succeed alone. European regulatory frameworks and ethical AI models must be combined with American production scale and venture velocity. This isn't just theory—it's a lesson conclusively demonstrated in cross-Atlantic productization projects successfully managed by No Friction and others who recognize this imperative.

Section 4: The Path Forward—A Call To Action

The time for half-measures has passed.

We stand at an inflection point where our choices will determine whether we maintain technological sovereignty or surrender it to more determined actors who understand the stakes.

We must act decisively across multiple fronts—now. The following recommendations aren't merely suggestions—they are imperatives:

  1. Fund Productization, Not Just Prototypes

    Establish federal (and EU-level) funding vehicles that specifically target the late-stage engineering and hardening of R&D into production-grade assets capable of deployment at scale [Porter, 2023]. This requires reforming incentive structures that currently reward novelty over usability.

  2. Realign Incentives for Dual-Use Development

    Implement tax credits, grants, and guaranteed procurement pathways for technologies demonstrably engineered to solve both defense and civilian challenges [RAND, 2024]. The current bifurcated approach creates unnecessary inefficiencies and vulnerabilities.

  3. Build Transatlantic Product Coalitions

    Form US/EU public-private partnerships focused not merely on co-investment, but on co-productization—harmonizing compliance frameworks, ensuring interoperability, and creating shared standards that amplify rather than constrain innovation [EIT Digital Policy Paper, 2023].

  4. Deploy Agentic AI for Accelerated Productization

    Invest immediately in explainable, agentic AI frameworks purpose-built to accelerate use-case discovery, validation testing, and devops workflows (No Friction, 2025). These tools can dramatically compress the time-to-deployment while improving quality.

  5. Measure What Actually Matters

    Redefine success benchmarks to track "mission impact" and "resilience factors"—not just commercial metrics or academic citations (No Friction, 2025 Benchmarking). What we measure determines what we build.
