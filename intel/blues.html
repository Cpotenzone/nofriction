Executive Summary for Program Funding

Executive Overview

The Long Beach Blues Society (LBBS) is seeking support for our program initiatives that are designed to preserve blues heritage, educate future generations, and build stronger communities through music. This funding request focuses on operational resources to promote, develop, and produce our core programs, covering administrative costs, community outreach, and impact measurement systems.

As a cultural cornerstone in Long Beach, we have established effective programs including Blues in the Schools, Blues for Vets, and Blues for Seniors that collectively serve thousands of community members each year. To sustain and expand these programs, we require dedicated funding separate from our capital campaign efforts.

This executive summary outlines our mission, program details, funding requirements, and expected community impact.

Our Mission & Vision

"We at the Long Beach Blues Society act as a sounding board that amplifies opportunities by creating first-hand experiences with artists and professionals in the music industry. Our programs nourish the whole person by bringing to light artists' spirit, thoughts, and creative expression in a safe learning environment."

The LBBS is committed to building a community that leaves blues artists and the blues music community better than we found them. We bring people together to promote legacy, growth, understanding, and well-being through music, history, and cultural exposition.

Our Core Values:

  • Cultural preservation of essential American musical heritage
  • Intergenerational knowledge transfer and mentorship
  • Community engagement and social cohesion through music
  • Support for artists at all stages of their careers
  • Inclusive music education for underserved populations
  • Cross-cultural awareness and appreciation

Our Programs: A Place for Everyone

Blues in the Schools

A comprehensive educational program that brings blues musicians and educators into classrooms, offering interactive workshops on blues history, instrumentation, and performance. This program serves to inspire the next generation of musicians while preserving blues heritage.

Key Components:

  • In-school performances and workshops
  • Instrument introduction and basics
  • Blues history and cultural context
  • Student performance opportunities

Blues for Vets

A specialized program that brings live blues performances to veterans' facilities and provides therapeutic music experiences. This program acknowledges the healing power of music for those who have served our country.

Key Components:

  • Live performances at VA hospitals and facilities
  • Music therapy sessions
  • Instrument learning opportunities
  • Social connection through shared musical experiences

Blues for Seniors

This program brings blues performances and workshops to senior living facilities, fostering intergenerational connections and providing cognitive and emotional benefits through musical engagement.

Key Components:

  • Interactive performances at senior facilities
  • Musical reminiscence therapy
  • Intergenerational music sharing
  • Cognitive stimulation through music

Resource Center for Young Musicians

A comprehensive support system for young and emerging blues artists, providing access to instruments, rehearsal spaces, recording opportunities, and professional guidance.

Key Components:

  • Instrument and equipment acquisition
  • Rehearsal space access
  • Recording workshops and mentoring
  • Industry veteran summits and mentor matching
  • Legal and contractual guidance

Mental Wellness for Musicians

A dedicated initiative addressing the unique mental health challenges facing musicians, including performance anxiety, touring stress, and career transitions.

Mental Health Resources

Collaboration with mental health professionals specializing in performer wellness and arts-related challenges

Support Groups & Workshops

Regular sessions focusing on coping strategies, mindfulness, and specific challenges like post-touring depression and career transitions

Program Impact Goals (IMHP)

Decrease Social Isolation

Create musical communities that connect individuals across generations and backgrounds

Sustainability Plan

The requested funding will establish robust systems and processes that will enhance our future fundraising capabilities. By demonstrating measurable program impacts, we will be better positioned to:

Impact & Success Measurement

The Long Beach Blues Society is committed to measuring and demonstrating the real-world impact of our programs. Funding will support the development of comprehensive measurement systems to track the following outcomes:

Quantitative Metrics

  • Program Participation

    Number of students, veterans, seniors, and musicians served annually

  • Institutional Reach

    Number of schools, VA facilities, and senior centers engaged

  • Resource Distribution

    Quantity of instruments, equipment, and resources provided

  • Financial Efficiency

    Program cost per participant and resource utilization rates

Qualitative Outcomes

  • Well-being Improvements

    Participant-reported mental health benefits and social connection

  • Educational Impact

    Music knowledge acquisition and cultural appreciation development

  • Community Cohesion

    Strengthened intergenerational and cross-cultural relationships

  • Artist Development

    Career advancement and skill development for musicians
