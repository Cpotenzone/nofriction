Executive Summary

This dashboard analyzes shipping operations for February 2025, providing insights into transportation methods, vendor relationships, geographical patterns, and delivery timing. The analysis reveals opportunities to improve logistics efficiency, optimize shipping methods, and enhance vendor relationships.

Key Insight

'Logistics & Managed' shipments represent only 26% of volume but generate 73% of all savings.

Risk Area

'MFR Arranged' shipments (44% of total) yield almost no savings, representing significant missed opportunity.

Opportunity

Shifting 30% of 'MFR Arranged' to 'Logistics & Managed' could yield additional $500K+ annual savings.

Shipment Methods Analysis

Average Savings by Shipment Method

Relative Frequency of Shipment Methods

Key Findings & Recommendations

  • Method Disparity: 'Logistics & Managed' shipments average $631 in savings per load compared to just $5 for 'MFR Arranged'.
  • Volume vs. Value: Despite making up only 26% of shipments, 'Logistics & Managed' generates 73% of all savings.
  • Back Haul Efficiency: 'Back Haul' shipments offer moderate savings ($423 per load) and should be expanded where possible.
  • Recommendation: Shift higher-weight shipments from 'MFR Arranged' to 'Logistics & Managed' for an estimated $1.5M annual savings opportunity.

Weight & Savings Relationships

Relationship Between Shipment Weight and Savings

Key Findings & Recommendations

  • Weight-Savings Correlation: Higher weight shipments generally offer greater savings potential, especially in the 35,000-45,000 lb range.
  • Efficiency Threshold: Shipments under 10,000 lbs rarely generate significant savings, suggesting consolidation opportunity.
  • Outlier Performance: Several 40,000+ lb shipments achieve $8,000+ savings—these are primarily 'Logistics & Managed' with key vendors.
  • Recommendation: Implement minimum shipment weight policies (aim for 25,000+ lbs when possible) and consolidate smaller orders.

Vendor Performance Analysis

Top Vendors by Total Shipment Weight

Key Findings & Recommendations

  • Vendor Concentration: Top 10 vendors account for over 65% of total shipment weight, offering leverage for negotiation.
  • High-Expense Outliers: LAMB-WESTON and MARKON consistently have the highest shipping expenses (often $5,000-13,000 per load).
  • Contract Inconsistency: Many vendors show inconsistent shipping methods across their orders. For example, TYSON FOODS ships via both 'MFR Arranged' ($0 savings) and 'Logistics & Managed' (high savings).
  • Vendor Opportunities: Focus renegotiation efforts on MCCAIN FOODS, LAMB-WESTON, and MARKON to shift more volume to 'Logistics & Managed'.

Delivery Timing Analysis

Daily Delivery Volume - February 2025

Key Findings & Recommendations

  • Extreme Volume Concentration: First two weeks of each month show 80% of all shipments, with nearly zero weekend activity.
  • Weekday Patterns: Mondays and Tuesdays show the highest volume, creating operational bottlenecks and potential overtime costs.
  • Resource Underutilization: Weekends and latter half of month show minimal activity, indicating poor asset utilization.
  • Recommendation: Implement delivery rebalancing to shift 30% of first-week volume to weeks 3-4 and explore weekend delivery options for high-priority customers.

Geographic Distribution Analysis

Origin-Destination State Pairs by Frequency

Key Findings & Recommendations

  • Texas-Centric Network: TX→TX shipments dominate the network (40%+ of all shipments), forming a hub-and-spoke system centered on Dallas/Fort Worth.
  • High-Efficiency Corridors: Intra-Texas routes average $0.001-0.01/lb shipping cost, while cross-country routes cost $0.03-0.09/lb.
  • Optimization Target: GA→TX, NE→TX, and CA→OK routes have particularly high expenses but are frequently shipped via 'MFR Arranged'.
  • Recommendation: Consolidate smaller out-of-state shipments into full truckloads and negotiate standard 'Logistics & Managed' contracts for high-volume routes from GA, CA, and ID.

Strategic Recommendations

Short-Term Actions (0-3 Months)

  1. Immediate Contract Review: Identify top 20 vendors currently using 'MFR Arranged' shipping with weights over 25,000 lbs and convert to 'Logistics & Managed'.
  2. Load Consolidation Policy: Implement minimum shipment weight thresholds and combine smaller orders to reduce partial truckloads.
  3. Delivery Rebalancing: Shift 20% of Monday/Tuesday deliveries to Thursday/Friday to balance weekly volume and reduce labor spikes.
  4. Vendor Score Cards: Develop logistics efficiency scorecards for top 50 vendors and share results to drive improvement.

Long-Term Strategy (3-12 Months)

  1. Transport Mode Optimization: Develop multi-modal shipping strategies for long-haul routes (CA→TX, ID→FL) to reduce expenses.
  2. Contract Renegotiation: Target MARKON, MCCAIN FOODS, and LAMB-WESTON for comprehensive logistics program overhauls.
  3. Monthly Delivery Balancing: Implement incentives to spread volume more evenly throughout each month, reducing first-half concentration.
  4. Regional Hub Strategy: Evaluate potential for regional consolidation centers to optimize long-haul efficiency from high-volume origins.

Conclusion

The shipment data analysis reveals significant opportunities to optimize logistics operations and drive cost savings. By focusing on shipping method selection, load consolidation, delivery timing, and vendor management, the organization can potentially realize $2.45M - $3.35M in annual savings.

The most impactful single strategy is converting 'MFR Arranged' shipments to 'Logistics & Managed' for high-weight loads, which could yield up to $1.5M in annual savings with relatively straightforward implementation. This should be prioritized alongside load consolidation efforts to maximize near-term impact.

Implementing these recommendations would not only reduce direct shipping costs but also improve operational efficiency, reduce labor bottlenecks, and strengthen vendor relationships through more standardized logistics practices.
