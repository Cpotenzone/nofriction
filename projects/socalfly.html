Essential June Fly Patterns

Adams Dry Fly

The quintessential all-purpose dry fly. Perfect for SoCal's diverse mayfly hatches throughout June.

Sizes: 14-18
Best Time: Morning & Evening
Waters: All mountain streams

Pale Morning Dun

Essential for Eastern Sierra waters. PMD hatches are legendary on Hot Creek and Owens River.

Sizes: 16-20
Best Time: Late morning
Waters: Eastern Sierra streams

Blue Winged Olive

Reliable throughout the season. BWOs emerge on cloudy days and provide consistent action.

Sizes: 18-22
Best Time: Overcast conditions
Waters: San Gabriel forks, Kern River

Prince Nymph

The most versatile nymph pattern. Effective year-round in all SoCal waters.

Sizes: 12-16
Best Time: All day
Waters: Universal pattern

Hare's Ear Nymph

Classic mayfly nymph imitation. Essential for matching natural nymphs in mountain streams.

Sizes: 14-18
Best Time: Pre-hatch periods
Waters: All trout streams

Pheasant Tail Nymph

The most proven nymph pattern ever created. Indispensable for selective trout.

Sizes: 16-20
Best Time: During hatches
Waters: Technical waters

Pro Tips & Strategies

Approach Strategies

  • Fish upstream, stay low, avoid shadows on water
  • Use longer leaders in clear water (12-15 feet)
  • Match fly size to natural insects exactly
  • Fish pocket water between crowds

Reading Water

  • Focus on seams, eddies, and drop-offs
  • Look for structure: logs, rocks, undercuts
  • Watch for rising fish patterns
  • Note depth changes and current breaks

Environmental Awareness

  • Check fire restrictions and access roads
  • Carry bear spray in wilderness areas
  • Pack out all trash, practice Leave No Trace
  • Respect private property and closures

Essential Gear

  • 4-6 weight rods for versatility
  • Floating and sinking tip lines
  • Variety of tippet sizes (4X-7X)
  • Polarized sunglasses and sun protection
