Top 30 Short Hikes Along Highway 395

Eastern Sierra Nevada • Family-Friendly Trails • 1-3 Miles

Lone Pine to Lee Vining
Elevation: 4,000-8,500 feet
Duration: 30 minutes - 3 hours

Trail Difficulty Legend

Easy: Mostly flat, well-maintained trails
Moderate: Some elevation gain, good for families
Challenging: Steeper terrain, more effort required

Lone Pine & Alabama Hills Area

Dramatic rock formations with Mt. Whitney backdrop • Elevation: 3,700-4,500 feet

1. Mobius Arch Loop Trail

Easy
Distance: 1.2 miles
Elevation: 200 ft gain
Time: 45 minutes
Rating: 4.7/5

Most popular hike in Alabama Hills featuring the iconic natural arch with Mt. Whitney views. Well-marked loop trail through otherworldly rock formations.

Family Features: Stroller-friendly sections • Rock scrambling opportunities • Bathroom at trailhead • Perfect for photos
Trailhead: Whitney Portal Road, 2.5 miles west from Lone Pine, then Movie Road 1.5 miles

2. Shark Fin Loop Trail

Easy
Distance: 1.5 miles
Elevation: 150 ft gain
Time: 1 hour
Rating: 4.5/5

Less crowded alternative to Mobius Arch, featuring unique shark fin-shaped rock formation. Great for sunset photography and wildlife viewing.

Family Features: Quiet trail • Wildlife spotting • Climbing opportunities • 360-degree mountain views
Trailhead: Alabama Hills Recreation Area, accessible via Movie Road from Whitney Portal Road

3. Whitney Portal National Recreation Trail

Moderate
Distance: 2.5 miles
Elevation: 600 ft gain
Time: 1.5 hours
Rating: 4.4/5

Interpretive trail showcasing the transition from desert to alpine environments. Multiple viewpoints of Mt. Whitney and Alabama Hills.

Family Features: Educational signs • Creek crossings • Picnic areas • Restrooms at Whitney Portal
Trailhead: Whitney Portal, 13 miles west of Lone Pine on Whitney Portal Road

4. Alabama Hills Arch Loop

Easy
Distance: 1.8 miles
Elevation: 250 ft gain
Time: 1.2 hours
Rating: 4.6/5

Extended loop combining multiple natural arches and rock formations. Excellent for exploring and photography without crowds.

Family Features: Multiple exploration opportunities • Film location history • Safe climbing areas • Panoramic views
Trailhead: Various access points along Movie Road in Alabama Hills Recreation Area

Essential Planning Tips

Best Times to Visit

  • Late June - September: Most trails accessible
  • October - May: Lower elevation trails only
  • July - August: Peak wildflower season
  • September - October: Fall colors

Family Safety Tips

  • Bring extra water (1 liter per person minimum)
  • Sun protection essential at high altitude
  • Watch for altitude effects above 8,000 feet
  • Store food properly in bear country
